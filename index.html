The National Wild and Scenic Rivers System

Over the last 50 years, the National System has grown to 226 rivers and 13,412.5 miles. While the goal is to have representatives from every river type, much of the National System are the mountainous rivers of the West; Oregon has by far the most rivers designated. The forested rivers of New England are well represented, as are the wild, remote rivers of Alaska. Rare, though, are rivers designated across the entire southern half of the country; there are few rivers from desert Southwest and only one bayou. Rarer still are rivers from the Great Plains. However, the National System is just getting started.
